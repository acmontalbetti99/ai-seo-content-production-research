## 1. Kevin Indig — Growth Memo Newsletter (Selected Issues, 2024–2025)

**Source:** https://www.kevin-indig.com/growth-memo/  
**Format:** Weekly newsletter  
**Collected:** April 2026

### About Kevin Indig
Former VP SEO at Shopify (world's largest e-commerce platform), former Director of SEO at G2 and Atlassian. Now advises high-growth SaaS companies including Ramp, Bounce, Hims, Nextdoor, and Snapchat on organic growth. His Growth Memo newsletter is one of the highest-signal SEO publications because his frameworks come from operating at genuine enterprise scale.

---

### Key Newsletter Thread: "When AI Content Production Actually Works"

**AI content is high-ROI for:**
- FAQ and support documentation (low differentiation requirement, high volume)
- Product spec pages and feature descriptions
- Programmatic location/comparison pages at scale
- First-draft generation for topics where the editor is the actual intelligence layer

**AI content is low-ROI (or actively harmful) for:**
- Thought leadership and opinion pieces
- Original research and proprietary data analysis
- Case studies and client stories
- Anything where the author's direct experience is the core value proposition

**Key quote (paraphrased from Growth Memo):**
> "The bottleneck in most content programs isn't writing speed — it's strategic clarity about what to write. AI solves the wrong problem for most companies. They use it to produce more of the wrong content faster."

---

### Key Newsletter Thread: "SEO and AI at Shopify Scale"

Kevin's account of how AI-powered content production actually worked at Shopify:

- At Shopify's scale, manual optimization was impossible
- The AI pipeline: programmatic template generation + automated NLP scoring + human editorial review queue
- **The human layer never went away** — it moved from "writing" to "reviewing and flagging"
- The highest-value pages were always human-written + human-optimized
- Volume content was the domain of automation

**Implication for playbook:** Tier your content: Tier 1 = fully human, Tier 2 = AI-drafted + human edited, Tier 3 = programmatic/automated.

---

### Key Theme: "The New KPIs for SEO" (2025)

- Old KPIs: organic sessions, keyword rankings, organic revenue
- New KPIs: brand mention rate in AI responses, citation frequency across LLMs, branded search volume growth, Share of Voice in AI answers

## 2. Eli Schwartz — Product-Led SEO Applied to AI Content

**Source:** https://www.elischwartz.co/ + interviews (2024–2025)  
**Collected:** April 2026

### Core Framework: Product-Led SEO in the AI Content Era

**Eli's fundamental argument:**
Most companies approach SEO backwards:
1. They identify keywords they can rank for
2. They create content targeting those keywords
3. They wonder why it doesn't convert

The correct order:
1. Identify what problems your product solves
2. Find where people search for those problems
3. Create pages that solve the problem AND demonstrate the product

**How AI content production makes this worse:**
> "AI makes it cheaper to do SEO wrong at scale. Most companies are going to use AI to produce more content targeting keywords they could rank for, not keywords that connect to their actual buying journey."

---

### Eli's Taxonomy: What Content Survives AI Overviews

**Content that will get eaten by AI Overviews:**
- "What is X" informational articles
- "How to do Y" generic guides
- Comparison articles where the comparison is generic
- Any content that answers a question AI can answer from training data

**Content that survives:**
- Proprietary data ("We analyzed 10,000 customers and found...")
- First-person experience ("Here's what happened when we ran this experiment...")
- Product-led content (pages that solve problems using YOUR specific product)
- Integration/use-case pages tied to specific workflows
- Competitor comparison pages with genuine differentiation

---

### Eli on AI Overviews (2025)

> "AI Overviews are going to devastate content that was designed to intercept informational queries. If you built your SaaS blog entirely on 'what is X' and 'how to Y' articles, you're going to lose a lot of traffic in the next 2 years."

> "The content that survives: proprietary data, first-hand experience, product comparisons, integration guides. Everything else becomes an AI Overview."

**Implication for playbook:** Before using AI to produce content at scale, audit existing content for AI Overview vulnerability. Shift production budget toward the survivable categories.

## 3. Affiliate Summit 2026 — Lily Ray: State of AI and SEO

**Source:** https://www.affiliatesummit.com/blogs/the-state-of-ai-and-seo-in-2026-with-lily-ray  
**Date:** February 2026

Key statistics from Lily Ray's Affiliate Summit 2026 talk:

1. **Clicks will decline** — brand visibility becomes the new KPI
2. **SEO still powers AI** — ranking influences RAG citations
3. **Third-party reputation is critical** — AI trusts corroboration from multiple sources
4. **Multimodal content wins** — YouTube and social media are heavily cited in AI answers
5. **Monetization models must evolve** — influence > traffic as the primary outcome
6. **First-person reviews matter more than ever**
