## Host: Peec AI (Marius Meiners)

**Q: What will Google look like in 2026?**

Google has said at Google I/O that AI mode is the future. I do think AI mode will become the default, but it will look different from what we see today.

**Q: How should SEO professionals measure success with fewer clicks?**

The metrics are definitely changing. We're focusing more on brand mentions, impressions, being cited as a great company, and the sentiment around the brand — especially how the brand is positioned in large language model responses.

**Q: Are you worried people will flood the internet with junk content to rank in AI results?**

Yeah, I think we're already seeing that. It feels like the early days of Google. But over time, large language models will need to fight spam the same way Google has for 25 years. I've been advising people that while certain tactics might work right now, if they come across as spammy, they could hurt your brand long term, especially if it affects whether you're included in future training data.

**Q: Is SEO dead?**

I don't think SEO is dead. Much of what we've been doing for years — structuring content, understanding searcher intent, optimizing for machines — translates well to AI search. Many LLMs still reference traditional search rankings as part of their answer construction, so SEO remains foundational.

## Key Takeaways

1. New success metrics = brand mentions in LLM responses, citation frequency
2. AI content spam wave happening — LLMs will develop spam filters like Google did
3. SEO fundamentals transfer to AI search
4. LLMs still reference traditional search rankings — SEO remains foundational
