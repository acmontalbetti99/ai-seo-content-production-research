## Core Argument (distilled from LinkedIn + newsletter + interviews)

Eli's position on AI-powered SEO content production can be summarized as:

> **"Most companies are going to use AI to produce the wrong content faster."**

His framework:
1. Before using AI to scale content production, you need to know *which* content is worth producing
2. Most SaaS companies confuse "keywords we can rank for" with "content that drives pipeline"
3. AI makes the mistake of producing irrelevant content cheaper and faster — amplifying the underlying strategy problem

---

## Post 1 — Product-Led SEO in the AI Era

**Date:** 2024–2025

> "The biggest mistake I see companies make with AI content production: they automate the wrong job. They use AI to write faster. What they should be using AI for is to think harder about content strategy before writing anything."

> "If you couldn't rank for a keyword with a human-written article, publishing 100 AI articles on the same topic won't fix the problem. The issue is usually authority or relevance, not production volume."

**Why it matters:** Eli's product-led SEO framework is the strategic counter-weight to "scale with AI." The playbook needs both — what to produce (Eli) and how to produce it efficiently.

---

## Post 2 — AI Overviews and SaaS Strategy

> "AI O[erviews are going to devastate content that was designed to intercept informational queries. If you built your SaaS blog entirely on 'what is X' and 'how to Y' articles, you're going to lose a lot of traffic in the next 2 years."

> "The content that survives: proprietary data, first-hand experience, product comparisons, integration guides. Everything else becomes an AI Overview."

## GEO Framework (Generative Engine Optimization)

1. **Structured, citable content** beats wall-of-text
2. **Source authority** > keyword optimization for AI visibility
3. **Content freshness** is re-weighted for AI systems
4. **SEOFOMO** as intelligence layer (35k+ subscribers)

> "I created LearningAIsearch.com as a free learning resource for everyone trying to understand how to optimize for AI search engines - not just Google, but also ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini. The fundamentals - quality, relevance, authority - still apply. The *format* of how you express those fundamentals is what changes."
