## Study 1: 900,000 New Pages - How Much Is AI Content? (May 2025)

**Key findings:**
- 74.2% of newly created web pages contain AI-generated content
- Breakdown: 2.5% "pure AI", 71.7% "AI-assisted", 25.8% "pure human"

> "I was quite surprised by the 2.5% 'pure AI' number. The most fascinating insight is the dominance of AI-assisted content with a whopping 71.7%. It seems like using AI is becoming table stakes for content creation." -- Tim Soulo

> "What if the 25.8% of human-only content is what actually cuts through the noise? Simply because it feels different to all the regurgitated AI slop?" -- Tim Soulo

**Implication:** AI-assisted (not pure AI) is the new baseline. The differentiation opportunity is in the human editorial layer.

---

## Study 2: AI Search Visitors Convert 23x Higher (June 2025)

**Key data points:**
- AI search traffic = 0.5% of total visits to Ahrefs' website
- Those visitors generated 12.1% of all signups
- Conversion rate: AI search visitors convert **23x higher** than organic
- AI traffic grew ~9.7x over previous 12 months
- ChatGPT accounts for majority of AI-generated referral traffic

## Patrick Stox at Ahrefs Evolve 2025: Key Themes

1. **AI search extends, doesn't replace traditional search**
2. **AI assistants <1% of traffic - YouTube is bigger opportunity**
3. **Black hat era for AI assistants** - susceptible to manipulation, will get cleaned up
4. **Mapping SEO to AEO:** repurpose content, diversify traffic sources, create citable content
5. **AI traffic is desktop-heavy** vs organic search which is mobile-dominant

## Key Takeaways

1. 71.7% of new content is AI-assisted - pure AI is only 2.5%
2. AI search visitors convert 23x higher - citation > traffic
3. YouTube is a bigger opportunity than AEO right now
4. Traditional SEO remains foundational
5. Create citable content - data studies and original research
