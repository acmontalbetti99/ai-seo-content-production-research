## Post 1 — GEO vs SEO: Danny Sullivan at WordCamp US 2025

**Date:** ~September 2025  
**Engagement:** High (100+ comments)

> At WordCamp US Portland 2025, Danny Sullivan reminded the industry: "Good SEO is good GEO. Or AEO. Or AI SEO. Or LLM SEO. They're all just SEO." It was a humorous way of saying: don't get distracted by new acronyms.
>
> But here's what I think SEOs should take from this:
>
> 1. Old wine, new bottle. These acronyms highlight shifts in where SEO shows up (AI Overviews, vector-driven answers, entity-based retrieval). Execution evolves, but the foundation — relevance, crawlability, intent satisfaction — remains.
>
> 2. Two lenses for SEO. Timeless SEO: site health, technical hygiene, structured data, and clear content. Adaptive SEO: optimizing so your content is summarized in AI-driven results, not just ranked in blue links.
>
> 3. User intent is still the anchor. Whether through keywords, snippets, or LLMs, the ultimate filter hasn't changed: does your content answer the user's intent better than alternatives?

**Why it matters:** Nathan uses the Danny Sullivan quote to make a contrarian-but-grounded point — the "GEO" hype is mostly rebranding. The fundamentals still apply.
