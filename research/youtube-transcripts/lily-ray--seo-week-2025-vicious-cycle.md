## Overview

Lily Ray closed out SEO Week 2025 reflecting on 15 years in SEO and the vicious cycle where SEOs discover new tactics, scale them, and trigger Google crackdowns.

## Transcript (Partial)

**Lily Ray:** If there's one thing you learn after doing SEO for 15 years, it's that SEO tends to follow somewhat of a predictable cycle — kind of a vicious cycle.

SEOs find some type of cool tactic for exploiting search engines and getting organic traffic. Google is watching very carefully — in many cases, they're literally watching the posts we make on social media. And they work on systems to counteract and nullify those tactics.

**[On AI Content and March 2024 Update]**

All throughout 2023 and 2024, there were complaints that Google felt too full of AI content, too full of spammy content, parasite SEO. And Google said, buckle up. We're working on it.

During the March 2024 core and spam update, which lasted 45 days — the biggest update in Google's history — according to Google, it reduced unhelpful content in Google search results by 45%.

A lot of it was companies who found opportunities and exploits where they could do programmatic SEO strategies and capture all the possible ways that people are searching with AI and generate as many pages as possible — and it was working really, really well for them.

**[On AI Overviews and Click Decline]**

AI overviews obviously cut into organic click through rates in a pretty dramatic way. This requires a shift in mindset. You can do great SEO without being stuck in this perpetual cat and mouse game.

## Key Takeaways

1. AI content at scale = the current gold rush and the next crackdown target
2. March 2024 proved it: 45% reduction in unhelpful content
3. AI Overviews reducing organic clicks — shift to brand visibility and citations
4. New frontier = authenticity, original research, thought leadership
5. External platforms (Reddit, YouTube, LinkedIn) dominate AI citations
